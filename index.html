About Me

This is a website that I have designed and created myself to share the projects that I have been working on and what I am currently working towards.

I learnt how to code during lockdown out of self-motivation. I started with Python and created a basic 2D platformer until I wanted to move to 3D. I then followed several online tutorials learning C# to create my first 3D game, The Sphere Game, with Unity. Since then, I have experimented with Unity to create more video games and software. I am still working on other games and projects building upon my knowledge of programming.

I bought a DJI Mini SE drone in 2022 for our holiday to Wales that Summer. Since then, I have been practising flying in different locations and I have used my drone to film the purpose work for my Silver Duke of Edinburgh award. I took my drone abroad for the first time when we went on holiday to Kefalonia, Greece and it was at this point that I started seriously researching drone laws and licensing. I successfully passed the Flyer ID test with a score of 38/40 (a pass was 30) meaning that I can legally fly heavier drones in limited areas. I have been working through a course with UAVHUB to obtain the A2 CofC (Certificate of Competency) which would allow me to fly commercially and closer to people and residential/recreational/industrial areas with heavier drones. I have not yet achieved the A2 CofC and do not intend on doing so as the qualification is only valid for 5 years and, due to the UK leaving the EU, drone laws in this country will change from 1st January 2026. Despite this, I still wish to educate myself as best I can to be a safe and well informed drone pilot as I understand that the drone industry is an ever changing environment with heavier, more dangerous drones being so easily accessible to the general public. This is why I am always trying to be on the correct side of the law and to be a respectful and safe drone pilot.

I have been playing the piano since 2019 where I first attempted to play "Three Blind Mice" on my grandma’s piano for a £5 bet. I then started getting lessons and I worked towards Grade 1 where I achieved a distinction. This is the only qualification I have obtained for the piano as I decided I would rather play pieces I want to play and not what the exam board wanted me to play. I went through a phase of composing, if you can even call it that, however I have moved away from creating my own music and moved towards classical pieces and, more recently, piano covers of well-known songs.

I believe that my love for playing the piano stems from my ability to move freely through contemporary dance. During 2021, I had an operation to my knee making me unable to dance for 9 months. This set me back due to muscle loss throughout that period, but it drove my passion for the piano as it was as if I was dancing with my fingers on the keys. In a way, playing the piano was my way of expressing my emotions and I still get a huge amount of joy out of listening to and playing the piano. Music and the performing arts has played a large part in my life, and I hope to inspire others to learn a musical instrument or to encourage other keen male dancers to thrive in what they love doing.
